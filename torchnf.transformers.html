torchnf.transformers

Module containing parametrised bijective transformations and their inverses.

Transformation functions take an input tensor and one or more tensors that parametrise the transformation, and return the transformed inputs along with the logarithm of the Jacobian determinant of the transformation. They should be called as

output, log_det_jacob = transform(input, params, context)

In maths, the transformations do

\[x, \{\lambda\} \longrightarrow y = f(x ; \{\lambda\}), \log \left\lvert\frac{\partial y}{\partial x} \right\rvert\]

The inverse of these transformations are

\[y, \{\lambda\} \longrightarrow x = f^{-1}(y ; \{\lambda\}), \log \left\lvert\frac{\partial x}{\partial y} \right\rvert\]

Thanks to the inverse function theorem, the log-Jacobian-determinants for the forward and inverse transformations are related by

\[\log \left\lvert \frac{\partial x}{\partial y} \right\rvert = -\log \left\lvert \frac{\partial y}{\partial x} \right\rvert\]
class torchnf.transformers.AffineTransform

Bases: Transformer

Performs a pointwise affine transformation of the input tensor.

The forward and inverse transformations are, respectively,

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}x \mapsto y = x \odot e^{-s} + t\\y \mapsto x = (y - t) \odot e^{s}\end{aligned}\end{align} \]
\[\log \left\lvert \frac{\partial y}{\partial x} \right\rvert = \sum_i -s_i\]

where \(i\) runs over the degrees of freedom being transformed.

The params argument to forward() and inverse() should be a tensor with \(s\) and \(t\) stacked, in that order, on the 1 dimension. The parameters will be split as

s = params[:, 0]
t = parmas[:, 1]

class torchnf.transformers.Rescaling

Bases: Transformer

Performs a pointwise rescaling of the input tensor.

The forward and inverse transformations are, respectively,

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}x \mapsto y = x \odot e^{-s} \, ,\\y \mapsto x = y \odot e^{s} \, .\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

The logarithm of the Jacobian determinant is

\[\log \left\lvert \frac{\partial y}{\partial x} \right\rvert = \sum_i -s_i\]

where \(i\) runs over the degrees of freedom being transformed.

class torchnf.transformers.Translation

Bases: Transformer

Performs a pointwise translation of the input tensor.

The forward and inverse transformations are, respectively

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}x \mapsto y = x + t \, ,\\y \mapsto x = y - t \, .\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

There is no change in the volume element, i.e.

\[\log \left\lvert \frac{\partial y}{\partial x} \right\rvert \log \left\lvert \frac{\partial x}{\partial y} \right\rvert = 0\]
